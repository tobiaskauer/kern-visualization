# Bar Chart

## When to use

Use a bar chart when comparing discrete categories where order does not inherently matter — for example, survey responses, product comparisons, or performance across regions. Horizontal bars are especially well-suited when category names are long, as there is more horizontal space for labels than in a column chart.

## When NOT to use

Do not use a bar chart for time-series data — prefer a line chart when the x-axis represents continuous time. Avoid it for part-of-whole comparisons (use a stacked bar chart instead), and do not use it for continuous distributions (use a histogram).

## Don't — too many bars

Avoid using a bar chart with more than 12 categories. When there are too many bars the chart becomes difficult to read and compare. Consider grouping, filtering, or switching to a table.
